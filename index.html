    Development Monitors is an interdisciplinary consulting company that designs and implements sustainable solutions across multiple fields and sectors. We also leverage low-cost and open-source hardware and soft-ware that can be maintained by our local partners.​

    About us

    Development Monitors is a US-registered company founded in 2017 by two experienced international development specialists: Dr. James Weeks and Mr. Umesh Tiwari. At its’ core is a development engineering company focused on tailoring and applying innovative strategies and technologies, including drones and weather stations, artificial intelligence and machine learning models, geospatial software and high-resolution satellite imagery, to holistically solve development challenges across a wide range of fields and sectors. With an office in the greater Washington, DC area and in New Delhi, India, along with a pool of experienced consultants stationed across the globe, the Development Monitors team consists of experienced international and national engineers, social scientists, and hardware and software developers.

    Development Monitors works closely with international donors, national and local governments, non-governmental organizations, and universities in Europe, Central/South Asia, the Middle East, and the United States to implement a wide variety of applied research and international development projects.​ ​ Since 2018, Development Monitors has partnered with The Washington Center to host over 60 undergraduate and graduate students in computer science, engineering, and international relations from ten countries for three to four-month full-time internships.​

    What We Do

    Development Monitors provides donors and governments with a range of services across multiple fields and sectors:​

    • Water Resource Management

      This World Bank Nepal project analyzed multiple watersheds in the Birendranagar municipality and neighboring Surkhet valley. Water sources were identified and mapped in critical watershed areas with an open-source georeferenced asset registry using aerial and satellite imagery in order to design protec-tive steps for disaster risk mitigation.​

    • Water Supply Diagnostic and Decision Support

      The objective of this World Bank assignment was to i) develop and pilot a georeferenced diagnostic and decision support tool for planning, maintaining, and restoring water supply systems in high seismic risk zones in Aizawl and Shimla, India, and ii) support knowl-edge-sharing of the tools among SAR countries and relevant stakeholders.​

    • Remote Monitoring, Sensing, and Verification

      This German Bank for Reconstruction (KfW) project had two two objectives: i) Inform KfW manage-ment on strategic opportunities and limitations using remote monitoring, sensing, and verification approaches in fragile and conflict-affected areas, and ii) Provide KfW staff with guidance to use remote monitoring, sensing, and verification methods and tools.​

    • Drone-based Community Assessment and Disaster Risk Recovery

      In this research project conducted with the Virginia Tech Uncrewed Systems Lab, a drone was used to capture aerial imagery of an area and reconstructed to produce three-dimensional models, digital elevation models, and orthophotos stitched together for flood modeling and damage assessment.​

    • Community Based Disaster Risk Management

      This World Bank project implemented a community-based disaster risk management life-cycle in six vulnerable Afghan communities. The primary object-tives involved i) assessing potential hazards, ii) developing community risk profiles, and iii) identifying community-driven mitigation measures.​

    • Climate-resilient Infrastructure Pre-feasibility and Investment Planning

      This World Bank project i) reviewed climate risk assessments for seven Yemen cities, ii) identified cli-mate-resilient infrastructure solutions to mitigate effects on vulnerable pop-ulations, and iii) prepared pre-feasi-bility and investment plans for select solutions covering technical, social, environmental, and economic aspects.​

    • Climate-resilient Infrastructure Planning and Design Capacity Building

      The objective of this World Bank project was to scale up climate resiliency efforts in multiple Afghanistan government ministries and build key engineering staff capacity to plan and design resilient infrastructure projects as well as strengthen government capacity for more effective climate-resilient project screening and prioritization.​

    • Water Supply and Sanitation Evaluation

      The objective of this World Bank Yemen assignment was to assess the functionality status of 55 rehabilitated water supply and sanitation schemes, their perfor-mance, sustainability, and resil-ience, and provide recommenda tions for their continued operation and future project financing in a fragile context compounded by the impact of climate change.​

    • Solid Waste Management Evaluation

      The objective of this World Bank Yemen assignment was to i) collect baseline data and design an output-based aid project to incen-tivize service providers to expand primary collection to underserved areas, ii) improve city-wide waste collection, and iii) identify opportu-nities to improve conditions for women and marginalized groups working in the waste cycle.

    • School Infrastructure Evaluation

      The primary objectives of this World Bank Afghanistan assignment was to i) provide a technical and economic analysis on the retrofitting of 42 existing schools, priori-tized by Government of Afghanistan, ii) leverage the multi-hazard risk assessment and government data on existing schools to develop retrofitting guidelines for other schools found at risk of natural hazards, and (iii) build client capacity to assess and retrofit schools.​

    • Low Carbon Development Assessment

      This World Bank Yemen project i) collected data from the water and sanitation, urban mobility, solid waste management, energy and housing sectors required for the World Bank CURB tool, and ii) carried out inclusive and gender segregated community consulta-tions to revalidate prioritized low carbon development interventions proposed by the World Bank.​

    • Value Chain Assessment

      In this World Bank assignment, we conducted i) a rapid mapping and assessment Yemen’s construction sector value chain, include manufacturing, contracting, and related service firms, relevant to reconstruction demand, and ii) identified development options for government and partners to strengthen Yemeni firms’ capabilities to service reconstruction demand. ​

    • Agriculture Productivity Assessment

      This study updated the World Bank's understanding of agriculture productivity and food security in Yemen. The study focused on domestic agri-food production potential and the role of the agri-food sector in improving nutrition. The resulting agriculture and livestock-related recommendations included public and private sector actions to reduce food and nutrition insecurity. ​

      Drone-Based Community Assessment, Planning, and Disaster Risk Management for Sustainable Development

      Accessible, low-cost technologies and tools are needed in the developing world to support community planning, disaster risk assessment, and land tenure.
