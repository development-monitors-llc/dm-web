Monitor. Analyze. Act.

Development Monitors is a global, interdisciplinary consulting company employing engineers, economists, and social scientists working across multiple fields and sectors: agriculture, disaster risk management, climate-resilient infrastructure, open-source hardware and software, remote sensing, water resource management.

What We Do - Project Focus Areas

  • Agriculture

    This study updated the World Bank's understanding of agriculture, food, and nutrition security in Yemen. It focused on the potential of domestic agri-food production and the role of the agri-food sector in improving nutrition. The study recommendations included actions focused on breaking the cycle of food and nutrition insecurity.

  • Water Resource Management

    This World Bank project focused on watersheds within the Birendranagar, Nepal municipality. Existing watershed plans were reviewed, critical watershed areas for water source protection and climate resilience were identified and groundwork for further watershed management planning was conducted.​

  • Disaster Risk Management

    This World Bank project implemented an eight-step community-based disaster risk management lifecycle in six vulnerable Afghanistan communities. The primary development objective involved the assessment of potential hazards resulting in community risk profiles along with community-driven mitigation measures.

  • COP29: UN Climate Talks in Baku 2024

    The 29th UN climate talks took place in Baku in November 2024. Germanwatch was on site. Read our background stories, press releases, and blog posts on the climate conference in times of multiple crises.

  • Securing the Fundamental Rights of Tomorrow

    Along with more than 54,000 claimants, we are taking the German government to court. We are demanding a national climate policy that protects fundamental rights and liberties today and in the future.

    About us

    Development Monitors is a US-registered company founded in 2017 by two experienced international development specialists: Dr. James Weeks and Mr. Umesh Tiwari. At its’ core is a development engineering company focused on tailoring and applying innovative strategies and technologies, including drones and weather stations, artificial intelligence and machine learning models, geospatial software and high-resolution satellite imagery, to holistically solve development challenges across a wide range of fields and sectors. With an office in the greater Washington, DC area and in New Delhi, India, along with a pool of experienced consultants stationed across the globe, the Development Monitors team consists of experienced international and national engineers, social scientists, and hardware and software developers.

    Development Monitors works closely with international donors, national and local governments, non-governmental organizations, and universities in Europe, Central/South Asia, the Middle East, and the United States to implement a wide variety of applied research and international development projects.​ ​ Since 2018, Development Monitors has partnered with The Washington Center to host over 60 undergraduate and graduate students in computer science, engineering, and international relations from ten countries for three to four-month full-time internships.​

    Publications

    Drone Imagery Research Paper | 30 April 2021

    Drone-Based Community Assessment, Planning, and Disaster Risk Management for Sustainable Development

    Accessible, low-cost technologies and tools are needed in the developing world to support community planning, disaster risk assessment, and land tenure. Enterprise-scale geographic information system (GIS) software and high-resolution aerial or satellite imagery are tools which are typically not available to or affordable for resource-limited communities.
    Flood Analysis | 04 October 2022

    Post-Flood Analysis for Damage and Restoration Assessment Using Drone Imagery

    With natural disasters continuing to become more prevalent in recent years, the need for effective disaster management efforts becomes even more critical. Specifically, flooding is an extremely common natural disaster which can cause significant damage to homes and other property. In this article, we look at an area in Hurley, Virginia which suffered a significant flood event in August 2021.
